The Central Limit Theorem

Let X1…XnX_1 \dots X_nX1​…Xn​ form a random sample of size nnn from a distribution with finite mean and variance. Then for any fixed number XXX, as nnn increass to infinity, the probability that the standardized sample mean has a normal distribution is the normal CDF at XXX.

  • In other words, if the sample is large, the distribution is approximately normal.
  • Standardizing: If we subtract the mean and divide by standard deviation, the result variable has mean 0 and variance 1.

Estimation

A parameter θ\thetaθ is a constant indexing a family of distributions (for example, μ\muμ, σ2\sigma^2σ2 from the normal distribution, λ\lambdaλ from the exponential distribution, aaa and bbb from the uniform distribution, nnn and ppp from the binomial distribution.

An estimator θ∧\theta^{\wedge}θ∧ is a function of the random variables in a random sample, typically with useful properties that give us information about the distributions. (Example: mean).
